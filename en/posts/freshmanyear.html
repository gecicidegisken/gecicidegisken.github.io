Freshman Year In a Nuthsell

A brief review of my first year courses in computer engineering and what I learned as a freshman.
August 29, 2020

Here is a quick review of my first year of computer engineering: Big dreams, very good steps, no conclusion. Let me explain both spring and fall seasons and prove my point:

First Term

Since I studied prep school ( for English), getting used to university was not a problem for me. I just needed to adjust my department, computer engineering, which was quite easy because I studied hard in the summer before. What a nerd!

I had lots of physics and calculus classes. Most of them started at 8 in the morning. Still can not forget the pain, but the idea of "Dude, I rise before the sun just to do SCIENCE AND MATH" was a real satisfaction. No complains.

When I said I studied before, I meant my favorite course: Algorithms and Programming. Basically, and obviously we learnt programming and ended up with a project, which is a simple console application (peg solitaire) written in C. Sounds like a joke to me now but back then my brain had serious contractions to develop that project.

Another computer-related course was "Introduction to Computer Science". I learnt most fundamental terms like binary system, computer networks, data manipulation etc. It was pretty cool actually. I just wish I could study more for this subject and learn more besides the lecture.

So, that's it. I studied math, physics, computer science and programming in my first 5 months in university. However, I still had lots of free time, which I used as a self-time, or socializing-time. I know that I'll be more and more busy every year, so these free times were like a pre-break for me. :)
Sometimes I complained about the lecturers (when I had hard times with the lecture) which was not very proactive. Now I know that only person who is responsible with what's going on in my life is me. From now on, I'll be taking control and see what can I do to solve a problem instead of complaining.

Second Term

Every single year, every single semester, students say these famous words: "I'll study hard this time." Mostly, they start very good, but then give up. (Not proud of it.) But this time, I said these words and everything went great. (almost) I woke up at 7, attended lectures until 5 pm, and studied in the library for 3 hours after classes. I mean, I was doing really good. Most importantly, I was determined to keep going because I was having fun and satisfied with my work.
But then,
Voila!
Global pandemic came over, everybody went back their home, classes become online classes and exams were online which was a bad experience in my university. My motivation simply gone, I mean nobody knew what could happen next. But surprisingly I did good. Not my best, but under the circumstances, I consider myself successful.

Second term courses were almost the same as the previous one, but they were volume 2. Calculus 2, Programming 2 etc.
At programming, we studied C#. After all those console applications, making GUI with winforms was like magic to me. You have probably similar with the famous book Head First C#. Here is the Github link to my solution for the 2.Lab Project The Quest in the book. I enjoyed C# to be honest. And I still do.

Physics 2 and Calculus 2 were the harder versions of earlier. Linear Algebra was pretty fun. Also ,there were some obligatory courses like history or Technical English, not to mention how easy (maybe unnecessary) these are.

I had a study group before pandemic came over, and we tried to develop basic games on Unity. When I discovered I don't want to turn on my computer knowing I'll be working on Unity, I realized I don't want to be a game developer. Everybody says 'decide your path in CS' . It's almost impossible to decide especially you don't have experience. But now, I have game developing experince and at least I know what is not my path.

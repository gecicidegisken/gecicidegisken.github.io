Freshman Year In a Nuthsell

A brief review of my first year courses
in computer engineering and
what I learned as a freshman.

Second year in the faculty is almost starting, but here is a quick review of my first year of computer engineering: Big dreams, very good steps, no conclusion.

Term 1

Since I studied prep school ( for English), getting used to university was not a problem for me. I just needed to adjust my department, computer engineering, which was quite easy because I studied hard in the summer before. What a nerd!

I had lots of physics and calculus classes. Most of them started at 8 in the morning. Still can not forget the pain, but the idea of "Dude, I rise before the sun just to do SCIENCE AND MATH" was a real satisfaction.

When I said I studied before, I meant my favorite course: Algorithms and Programming. Basically, and obviously we learnt programming and ended up with a project, aka a simple console application written in C. Sounds like a joke to me now but back then my brain had serious contractions to develop that project.

Another computer-related course was Introduction to Computer Science. I learnt most fundamental terms like binary system, computer networks, data manipulation etc. It was pretty cool actually. I just wish I could study more for this subject and learn more besides the lecture.

So, that's it. I studied math, physics, computer science and programming in my first 5 months in university. However, I still had lots of free time, I used it as a self-time, or socializing-time. I know that I'll be more and more busy every year, so these free times were like a pre-break for me. :)
Sometimes I complained about the lecturers (when I had hard times with the lecture) which was not very proactive. Now I know that only person who is responsible with what's going on in my life is me. From now on, I'll be taking control and see what can I do to solve a problem instead of complaining.

Term 2

Every single year, every single semester, I say these famous words: "I'll study hard this time." Mostly, I start very good, but then give up. (Not proud of it.) But this time, this time everything was great. I woke up at 7, arrived school at 8, attended lectures until 5 pm, and studied in the library for 3 hours after classes. I mean, I was doing really good. Most importantly, I was determined to keep going because I was having fun.
But then,
Voila!
Global pandemic came over, everybody went back their home, classes become online classes and exams were online which was a bad experience in my university. My motivation simply gone, I mean nobody knew what could happen next. But surprisingly I did good. Not my best, but under the circums, I consider myself successful.

These term's courses were almost the same as the previous one, but they were volume 2. Calculus 2, Programming 2 etc.
At programming, we studied C#, after all those console applications, winforms was like magic to me. I enjoyed C# very much and I still do.

I had a study gropu before pandemic came over, and we tried to develop basic games on unity. When I discovered I don't want to turn on my computer knowing I'll be working on Unity, I realized I don't want to be a game developer. Everybody says 'decide your path in CS' . It's almost impossible to decide especially you don't have experience. But now, I have game developing experince and at least I know what is not my path.
